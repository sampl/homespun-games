Homespun games

A homespun game is a game that requires only common household objects to play. No digital devices are allowed.

Perfect for friends and family during parties, travel, or just a quiet evening at home.

Exquisite corpse

Take turns drawing a part of a picture, but nobody sees the whole drawing until the end.

All ages

Flip cup

Drinking game where the first team to flip all their cups over with just a finger wins.

Adults only

Pictionary

Try to get your team to guess a secret word by drawing it

All ages

Fictionary

Try to guess which word definitions are real, and which were made up by other players

All ages

Hearts

Take turns discarding cards; lowest number of points wins

Teens and adults

Poker

Bet against each other to see who has the best hand

Teens and adults

Euchre

Teams of players play cards in "tricks", first team to ten wins

Teens and adults

Rummy

Add and discard cards from a pile, building sets in your hand to score points

Teens and adults

Paper Football

Flick an origami football across the table. Score points when it hangs over the edge (but doesn't fall off).

All ages

Tic-tac-toe

Take turns drawing X's and O's on a grid, trying to get three in a row

All ages

Concentration

Try to find card pairs by turning them over and remembering which card is where.

All ages

Hangman

Try to find a secret word by guessing one letter at a time

All ages

Dots and Boxes

Take turns filling in one line on grid paper, trying to ________

All ages

Fishbowl

Write a bunch of words on paper slips. Players draw a word and give hints to their team, first by describing, then acting it out, then describing it with one word.

Teens and adults

Landmines

Spin a quarter, take a drink, and pick up the quarter with one hand. When you finish a beer, place it on the table to make quarter spinning harder for other players.

Adults only

Charades

Get your teammates to guess a word by silently acting it out

All ages

Telestrations

The first player writes down a word, the next guesses the word based on the drawing, the next player draws the word, etc

Teens and adults

Mao

Card game where players learn the rules as they play

Teens and adults

Would you rather

Take turns asking each other questions like "would you rather be a duck or a horse"?

All ages

Truth or dare

Take turns choosing between answering a question honestly or doing a dare that other players choose.

Kids and teens

I spy

Pick something in the room. Other players try to guess it asking yes or no questions.

All ages

Twenty questions

Choose an object anywhere in the world. Other players try to guess it asking only yes or no questions.

All ages

Two truths and a lie

Tell other players two facts about you and one lie; they try to guess the lie.

All ages
